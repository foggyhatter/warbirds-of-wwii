Focke-Wulf Fw 190

The Fw 190 was one of the primary fighter planes of the Luftwaffe, along with the Messerschmitt Bf 109.

At its introduction in 1941, the Fw 190 outmatched the Spitfire in nearly every way. Its 20mm machine guns were more powerful and its newer radial engine allowed it to go faster. The British Spitfire was unable to outmatch the Fw 190 for the first year of the fighter's active operation, as they were unable to obtain a specimen for study. This problem was solved when a Luftwaffe pilot landed at an RAF airfield after mistaking it for one in France. The Fw 190 remained one of the most effective aircraft in Germany's arsenal throughout the second World War. A later variant of the craft, designed for bomber-killing, traded speed and maneuverability for armor and heavy weapons. This design was used to counter the U.S.A.A.F.'s B-17 Flying Fortress, which was too heavily armed to be fought by normal fighters.

Mitsubishi A6M Zero

The Mistubishi A6M Zero was one of the most feared Japanese fighters of World War II.

The A6M Zero has a coloful history, having taken part inc all major battles involving the Japanese, from the hostilities between Japan and China, to Pearl Harbor, to the final years of the war in the Pacific. The Zero was far more acrobatic than the Allied fighters, and so achieved many victories in the early years of the war, especially where Allied fighters were too few to put to effective use the strategies needed to counter them.

Fiat G.50 Freccia

The Fiat G.50 was an early fighter plane deployed by Italy in World War II.

Overall, the G.50 was a failure. The G.50 was unable to outmaneuver the Spitfire and other Allied fighters. The G.50 only met with success when relying heavily on surprise tactics. Multiple times a smaller number of Allied aircraft were able to easily escape pilots of the G.50, with little to no damage. Only in the hands of the most skilled pilots was the G.50 any threat, although the fighter did meet more success in the hands of the Finnish air forces in their war against the Soviet Union.
